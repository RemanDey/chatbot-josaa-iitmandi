# IIT Mandi — Coding Culture

## Overview

- Coding culture at IIT Mandi is present but not as deeply developed as IIT Delhi, Bombay, Kharagpur, or Hyderabad
- Growing CP community; some students reach Codeforces Specialist/Expert level
- Open-source contribution culture: nascent; individual students contribute but no institute-wide initiative at scale
- Hackathon participation: moderate; some teams participate in national hackathons
- The culture is improving year over year as placement awareness grows

## Competitive Programming

- Codeforces, LeetCode, HackerRank: actively used by motivated students
- No dominant institute-level CP club comparable to IIT Delhi's (as of 2024)
- Some student-run coding groups on WhatsApp/Discord exist informally
- ICPC participation: IIT Mandi teams have participated; reaching regional rounds is achievable but final rounds are rare
- Honest assessment: CP culture is not the strongest draw of IIT Mandi; self-driven students can build skills but will not find the same peer pressure/push as older IITs

## Placement-Oriented Coding Prep

- DSA (Data Structures & Algorithms) preparation is student-driven
- LeetCode-style grind is common among placement-aspirant students from 3rd year onward
- Coding bootcamps and prep groups form informally around placement season
- Some seniors mentor juniors through study groups
- Online platforms (InterviewBit, Striver's A2Z DSA) commonly used

## Development Culture

- Web development, app development, ML projects: individual students pursue actively
- Student clubs/groups exist for technical projects
- Elan (tech fest) and other events provide platforms for project showcases
- GitHub activity among motivated students: moderate
- Projects from coursework often extended to personal portfolios

## ML/AI/Data Science Culture

- Growing rapidly, especially with DSE branch establishment
- IIT Mandi iHub has AI/ML focus areas relevant to students
- Faculty research in ML/AI creates some project opportunities
- Kaggle participation: some students; not institutionally driven

## Key Honest Constraints

- Smaller peer pool means fewer high-level CP peers to benchmark against
- Remote location limits access to city-based hackathons/meetups
- Internet infrastructure is generally okay but not metro-level
- Limited industry presence on campus means less casual industry exposure
- Students who want a coding-intensive culture should understand they'll need to be more self-driven here

## What Works in Mandi's Favor

- Less social distraction (remote location can be productive for focused students)
- Faculty research projects provide applied coding experience
- Smaller batch means easier faculty interaction for research projects
- iHub initiatives provide some real-world project exposure
- Students who are self-motivated can differentiate themselves more easily than in larger IITs

## Coding-to-Placement Pipeline

- For CSE/DSE students: active LeetCode prep → on-campus placement in software roles is a realistic path
- For EE/EP/MnC students: transitioning to software requires deliberate additional effort
- Core branch students (ME, CE) transitioning to software: difficult; requires sustained 2-year effort on DSA + projects

## Clubs and Technical Communities

- Aavhan: Technical club (general)
- Parallax: Photography/tech overlap
- Robotics/AI clubs: exist in informal capacity
- Note: Club scene is smaller than older IITs; clubs may be less active during off-peak periods
