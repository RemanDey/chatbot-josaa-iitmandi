# IIT Mandi — JoSAA Cutoffs Overview

## Data Source and Reliability

- Data sourced from JoSAA official portal (josaa.nic.in) — published after each counseling year
- Opening ranks (OR) and Closing ranks (CR) are for Round 6 (final round) unless specified
- Category-wise data available on JoSAA portal; general category (OPEN) discussed primarily
- **Verified against JoSAA 2023 and 2024 data; 2025 data to be verified when available**
- Cutoffs change every year; use this as directional guidance, not absolute targets

## Understanding JoSAA Cutoff Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Opening Rank (OR) | Highest JEE Advanced rank that got seat in round 1 |
| Closing Rank (CR) | Lowest JEE Advanced rank that got seat in final round |
| CRL | Common Rank List (Overall merit rank) |
| Category rank | Separate for OBC-NCL, SC, ST, EWS, PwD |
| Round-wise movement | Cutoff tightens through rounds 1→6 |

## General Category Closing Ranks — IIT Mandi (Approximate, 2023–2024)

| Branch | Approx CR (CRL, OPEN) | Trend |
|---|---|---|
| CSE | 8,000–12,000 | Most competitive at IIT Mandi |
| DSE | 10,000–15,000 | Slightly below CSE |
| EE | 12,000–18,000 | Second cluster |
| MnC | 13,000–18,000 | Similar to EE |
| ME | 16,000–22,000 | Third cluster |
| EP | 18,000–25,000 | Fourth cluster |
| CE | 22,000–30,000 | Least competitive |

**Important:** These are approximate ranges. Actual cutoffs vary by year, seat matrix, and candidate pool. Always check JoSAA official portal for exact data.

## Category-Wise Patterns

- OBC-NCL closing ranks: approximately 2.75–3x the General CR (rough rule of thumb)
- SC closing ranks: approximately 5–7x the General CR
- ST closing ranks: approximately 10–15x the General CR
- EWS closing ranks: approximately 1.2–1.5x the General CR
- PwD: significantly relaxed cutoffs

**Example (rough):** If CSE OPEN CR is ~10,000, OBC-NCL CR might be ~28,000, SC CR might be ~55,000. These are rough estimates — verify on JoSAA portal.

## Round-Wise Cutoff Movement

- Round 1: Most competitive (ranks up to CR 1 tight)
- Rounds 2–4: Modest upward movement in closing ranks as students withdraw for higher preferences
- Round 5–6: Final settling; closing ranks for last round are the published annual cutoffs
- Students with rank near closing rank: high anxiety in early rounds; final allotment is what matters
- Withdrawal and float options affect round-wise dynamics significantly

## Year-to-Year Variation

- CSE at IIT Mandi CR has been relatively stable (±1,000–2,000 ranks over years)
- EE, ME, CE have more year-to-year variation
- New branches (DSE) may show more volatility as market perception evolves
- Gender-neutral pools have separate cutoffs in some categories

## Demand Hierarchy at IIT Mandi

1. CSE (highest demand)
2. DSE (second; newer but perceived as software-adjacent)
3. EE (third; traditional preference for circuits)
4. MnC (fourth; smaller but steady demand)
5. ME (fifth; core engineering; declining relative demand)
6. EP (sixth; niche demand; highly specific students)
7. CE (seventh; lowest demand)

## Home State Quota

- IIT Mandi has Home State (Himachal Pradesh) quota seats
- HP students get a designated quota within state — check JoSAA seat matrix for exact numbers
- This does NOT significantly change the overall cutoff analysis for non-HP students

## Gender-Neutral Pool (Supernumerary Female Seats)

- IITs now have female supernumerary seats to improve gender diversity
- Female candidates may get seats at lower than published CRL closing ranks
- Actual mechanism: separate pool with female-only candidates; higher closing rank pool may be filled first
- Female candidates should check gender-pool cutoffs separately on JoSAA portal
