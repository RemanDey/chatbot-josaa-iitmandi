# CSE vs DSE — Decision Notes

Purpose: compare Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) with Data Science & Engineering (DSE).

Core differences
- CSE: broad software engineering foundation — algorithms, systems, software design.
- DSE: data-focused — statistics, ML, data pipelines, applied modeling.

Career & placement
- CSE: wider recruiter pool across product, infra, and backend roles.
- DSE: targeted roles in data engineering, ML engineering, analytics, and applied research — often with smaller but growing recruiter pools.

When recommend CSE
- Student wants maximum optionality across software roles, enjoys algorithms and systems design.

When recommend DSE
- Student primarily wants ML/data careers, enjoys statistics and modeling, and is willing to take domain-specific electives.

Transition notes
- DSE graduates can move to software roles but may need stronger systems design and coding portfolios; CSE graduates can pivot to data roles with ML projects and coursework.

Agent guidance
- Ask whether the student prefers ML/statistics work or broader software engineering; map to role examples (ML engineer vs backend engineer).

End.
# CSE vs DSE at IIT Mandi

**CSE (Computer Science & Engineering):**
- Broader CS foundation (systems, theory, software)
- Best for software/product roles
- Slightly higher placement % and packages

**DSE (Data Science & Engineering):**
- Focus on ML, AI, statistics, data engineering
- Best for data science/analytics roles
- Placement % and packages close to CSE

**Summary:**
- CSE is more flexible; DSE is more data/ML focused. Both have strong placements at IIT Mandi.
