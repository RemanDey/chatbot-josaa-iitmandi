# Old IIT Core vs Gen2 IIT Circuit — Notes

Purpose: contextualize differences between traditional "core" identities and modern interdisciplinary "Gen-2" circuit/EE tracks at newer IITs.

Overview
- Old IIT core: historically siloed departments (CSE, EE, ME, CE, CH) with strong disciplinary depth.
- Gen-2 circuit/EE: many newer programs blur hardware/software boundaries, emphasize interdisciplinary projects, and offer flexible electives.

Practical counseling implications
- Students at Gen-2 programs may get more interdisciplinary exposure but should still check core course depth for PSU or deep research goals.
- For placement, Gen-2 flexible tracks often increase optionality but individual student effort (projects/internships) remains the primary signal.

Agent guidance
- Don't assume modern program names imply weaker fundamentals; always check specific course lists and research/internship pipelines before advising.

End.
# Old IIT Core vs Gen2 IIT Circuital Branches

**Old IIT Core (e.g., IIT Bombay/Delhi ME/EE/CE):**
- Stronger brand, alumni, and core job pipeline
- Better for PSU, research, and core industry
- Lower placement % in core, but higher quality

**Gen2 IIT Circuital (e.g., IIT Mandi CSE/EE/DSE):**
- Newer, more software/data focus
- Higher placement % in software/analytics
- Brand is growing, but not as strong as old IITs

**Summary:**
- Old IIT core is best for core/PSU/research. Gen2 IIT circuital is better for software/data. Tradeoff depends on your goals.
