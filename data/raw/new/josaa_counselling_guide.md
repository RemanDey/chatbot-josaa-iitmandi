---

## What Is JoSAA?

**JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority)** is the centralized system that allots seats across:
- All 23 IITs (using JEE Advanced rank)
- All 31 NITs (using JEE Main rank)
- All 26 IIITs (using JEE Main rank)
- GFTIs — Government Funded Technical Institutes (JEE Main rank)

**IIT admission = JEE Advanced rank only.** JEE Main rank is irrelevant for IITs.
**NIT/IIIT admission = JEE Main rank only.** JEE Advanced rank is irrelevant for NITs.

---

## JoSAA Process — Step by Step

```
Appear for JEE Main → Qualify for JEE Advanced → Appear for JEE Advanced
            ↓
      Get JEE Advanced AIR (All India Rank)
            ↓
    JoSAA Registration opens (June/July)
            ↓
   Fill preference list (IIT + branch combinations, ordered by priority)
            ↓
   6 Rounds of seat allotment (July–August)
   After each round: Accept / Upgrade / Withdraw
            ↓
   Final seat locked → Document verification → Join institute
```

**Key dates (approximate, changes yearly):**
- JEE Advanced results: June
- JoSAA registration + preference filling: June–July
- Round 1 allotment: Late June / Early July
- Rounds 2–6: July–August (one round every 2–4 days)
- Final round: Usually Round 6 (mid-August)
- Reporting to institute: August–September

## Understanding Opening vs Closing Ranks

| Term | What It Means | Which One to Use for Planning |
|---|---|---|
| **Opening Rank** | Best AIR that got allotted in Round 1 | Don't plan based on this |
| **Closing Rank** | Last AIR allotted in Round 6 (final round) | **Use this for planning** |

**Example:** IIT Mandi CSE 2024 — Opening ~1,400, Closing ~3,118
→ If your rank is 2,500, you had a realistic chance but were not guaranteed in Round 1.
→ You likely got allotted in Round 4–5.

**Rule of thumb:** Your rank should be ≤ Closing Rank of your target IIT+Branch to have a realistic chance.

## Category-wise Rank Relaxation

IIT seats are reserved as per Government of India policy:

| Category | Seat Reservation | Approx Rank Multiplier vs General |
|---|---|---|
| General (CRL / OPEN) | ~40% seats | 1× (baseline) |
| OBC-NCL | 27% seats | ~2–3× relaxation |
| EWS | 10% seats | ~3–4× relaxation |
| SC | 15% seats | ~4–6× relaxation |
| ST | 7.5% seats | ~6–10× relaxation |
| PwD | 5% horizontal | Additional relaxation within each category |

**Female Supernumerary Seats:** ~20% additional seats for female candidates. Separate closing ranks — usually 1.5–3× more relaxed than male general closing ranks.

**Example:** IIT Delhi CSE General CRL closing rank ~126
- OBC-NCL: ~89 (OBC rank, not CRL)
- SC: ~40–50 (SC rank)
- ST: ~15–20 (ST rank)

# SECTION F: JoSAA STRATEGY TIPS

## Preference List — How to Fill It Right

**Golden Rule:** Fill your preference list in **strictly decreasing order of your actual preference, not based on what you think you'll get.** JoSAA's algorithm is designed so you can safely put dream options first without risk of losing a lower preference.

**How the algorithm works (simplified):**
1. You fill 50+ preferences ordered 1 to N
2. Each round, the system tries to allot your highest available preference
3. If allotted, you can either lock (accept without upgrade) or float (accept but try to upgrade in next round)
4. In the next round, if a better preference frees up, you get upgraded automatically (if you chose float)
5. Your current allotment is only lost if you get a better one — it is never taken away arbitrarily

**Floating vs Freezing:**
- **Float:** Keep your current seat but try to upgrade. Use this if you want a better seat.
- **Freeze:** Accept current seat as final. Use only when you are satisfied.
- **Slide:** Accept seat in the same institute but try for a better branch at that institute.

## How Many Preferences to Fill

Fill **at least 20–30 preferences** covering:
- Aspirational (2–3 options where you need to be lucky)
- Realistic (5–8 options that match your rank)
- Safe (5–10 options you'll definitely get)
- Emergency (5+ options at lower-closing-rank IITs)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong |
|---|---|
| Filling only 5–10 preferences | You may not get any if all are too competitive |
| Ordering based on "what you'll get" instead of preference | Defeats the purpose of the algorithm |
| Ignoring branch fit for IIT brand | A bad-fit branch at IIT Bombay ≠ happiness |
| Not checking category-specific closing ranks | General ranks don't apply to you if you're OBC/SC/ST |
| Forgetting female supernumerary quota (if female) | Separate closing ranks, often more relaxed |
| Not checking current year cutoffs | Ranks shift year-to-year — old data misleads |
| Withdrawing after a good allotment to "wait for better" | You lose your seat permanently if you withdraw |

## The IIT Brand vs Branch Dilemma

Common question: "Should I take Civil Engineering at IIT Bombay or CSE at IIT Mandi?"

**There's no universal answer.** Consider:
- **For tech/software career:** CSE at any IIT opens doors; IIT Mandi CSE is well-placed. IIT Bombay Civil may feel like a mismatch if you want software.
- **For core engineering career:** IIT Bombay Civil has significantly better core placements, PSU options, and alumni network.
- **For research:** IIT Bombay/Madras research resources > IIT Mandi, regardless of branch.
- **For placement packages in software:** IIT Mandi CSE median (₹22 LPA) is competitive; IIT Bombay Civil median is lower.

**General guidance:**
- If the branch interests you genuinely → choose better IIT even if branch rank is lower
- If the branch doesn't interest you → choose the branch you want at the best IIT you can get it
- Never choose a branch purely for "prestige" at an IIT if you have zero interest in it

## Category-Wise Counselling Notes

**OBC-NCL:**
- Use OBC rank (not CRL) to compare against OBC closing ranks
- OBC-NCL ranks are usually 2–3x more relaxed than CRL ranks
- Example: IIT Mandi CSE OBC closing rank ~1,427 (OBC rank)

**SC:**
- SC closing ranks are 4–6x more relaxed than CRL
- IIT Mandi CSE SC closing rank: ~761 (SC rank)

**ST:**
- Most IIT branches are accessible at lower ST ranks
- IIT Mandi CSE ST closing rank: ~363 (ST rank)

**EWS:**
- EWS ranks are typically 3–4x more relaxed than CRL
- Verify income certificate validity (< ₹8 lakh/year, no large asset holdings)
