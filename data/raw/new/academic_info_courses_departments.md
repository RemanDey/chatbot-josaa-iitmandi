## 1. About IIT Mandi

- **Full Name:** Indian Institute of Technology Mandi
- **Established:** 2009 (one of the newer second-generation IITs)
- **Type:** Autonomous research university (Institution of National Importance)
- **Location:** Kamand Valley, Mandi district, Himachal Pradesh (~14 km from Mandi town)
- **Campus:** On the left bank of Uhl River, at Kamand and Salgi villages
- **Rankings:** Ranked #14 for Overall (India Today 2023); among top IITs in research output

## 4. B.Tech Curriculum Structure

IIT Mandi follows an **interdisciplinary B.Tech curriculum**. All B.Tech students share a common core regardless of branch.

### 4.1 Core Credit Distribution (All Branches)

| Domain | Core Credits |
|---|---|
| Basic Sciences (Math, Physics, Chemistry, Biology) | 25 |
| Computing & Electrical Engineering | 21 |
| Mechanical Engineering & Material Sciences | 21 |
| Humanities & Social Sciences | 13 |
| Humanities & Social Sciences (Optional) | 5 |
| Branch-specific core | Varies |

### 4.2 Sample Core Courses (All Branches)

- Understanding Biotechnology and its Applications (IC 136) — 3-0-0-3
- Signals and Systems (IC260) — 2.5-0.5-0-3
- Continuum Mechanics (IC242) — 2.5-0.5-0-3
- Organizational Behavior (HS403) — 3-0-0-3
- Mathematics courses (Calculus, Linear Algebra, etc.)
- Introduction to Programming
- Engineering Drawing
- Communication Skills

### 4.3 Credit Format

IIT Mandi uses the **L-T-P-C format**:
- L = Lecture hours per week
- T = Tutorial hours per week
- P = Practical/Lab hours per week
- C = Credits

### 4.4 Semester Structure (Typical)

| Semester | Pattern |
|---|---|
| 1st & 2nd | Common for all branches — foundation courses |
| 3rd–6th | Branch-specific core + electives |
| 7th–8th | Major project / thesis / electives |

### 4.5 Class Timings

- Classes run from **8 AM to 6 PM** (occasionally up to 9 PM if syllabus behind)
- Each lecture: 50 minutes + 10-minute break
- Labs/practicals are scheduled separately

### 4.6 Assessment Pattern

For each course per semester:
- Quiz 1
- Quiz 2
- End-Semester Examination
- Assignments / Projects (varies by course)

## 7. Branch Change Policy

> **⚠️ IMPORTANT UPDATE (2024): IIT Mandi has DISCONTINUED branch change from 2024 onwards.**

- Effective 2024, IIT Mandi joined IIT Bombay, IIT Hyderabad, IIT Jammu, IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Dhanbad, IIT Dharwad, and IIT Bhubaneswar in **stopping the branch change option**.
- **Reason:** To reduce first-year academic stress and because the flexible elective curriculum already allows students to study across disciplines.
- Students joining from 2024 batch onwards **cannot change their branch**.

**Alternatives to branch change:**
- **Minor programmes:** Take 4 free electives from another department to earn a Minor
- **Double Major:** Some combinations available
- **Elective flexibility:** 40–50% of curriculum is elective-based

> For students who joined before 2024 and have questions about old branch change policies, contact academics@iitmandi.ac.in.

## 9. Academic Regulations — Key Points

### 9.1 Grading System

IIT Mandi follows the **10-point CGPA grading system**:

| Grade | Points |
|---|---|
| A+ (Outstanding) | 10 |
| A (Excellent) | 9 |
| B (Very Good) | 8 |
| C (Good) | 7 |
| D (Average) | 6 |
| E (Pass) | 5 |
| F (Fail) | 0 |

### 9.2 Minimum Requirements

- Minimum CGPA to continue: typically 5.0 (check ordinance for programme-specific requirements)
- Students below minimum CGPA may be placed on academic probation or asked to repeat courses

### 9.3 Attendance

- Minimum attendance requirement: **75%** in each course
- Below 75% may result in being detained from end-semester exam
- Medical leave with certificate is the only valid reason for attendance exemption

## 11. Frequently Asked Questions — Academics

**Q: What is the minimum CGPA to stay in IIT Mandi?**  
A: Minimum 5.0 CGPA (check programme-specific ordinance for exact requirements).

**Q: Can I change my branch at IIT Mandi?**  
A: No. Branch change was discontinued from 2024 onwards. Students joining 2024 batch or later cannot change branches.

**Q: How do I register for courses?**  
A: Through SAMARTH portal. Pre-registration opens before each semester.

**Q: What is the attendance requirement?**  
A: 75% minimum in each course. Below 75% may bar you from end-semester exam.

**Q: How many quizzes are there per course?**  
A: Typically 2 quizzes + 1 end-semester exam per course per semester, plus assignments/projects.

**Q: When do end-semester exams happen?**  
A: November–December (Odd Semester) and May–June (Even Semester). Check academic calendar.

**Q: How do I get transcripts?**  
A: Apply through SAMARTH portal or Academics Section.
