# IIT Mandi — Institute Overview

## Founding and Status

- Established: 2009 under the Institutes of Technology Act
- Category: Second-generation IIT (along with IIT Ropar, Patna, Jodhpur, Gandhinagar, Bhubaneswar, Hyderabad)
- Permanent campus: Kamand, Uhl River valley, Mandi district, Himachal Pradesh
- Altitude: ~900m above sea level; Himalayan terrain
- Nearest city: Mandi town (~13 km); nearest major city: Chandigarh (~200 km)
- Institute code (JoSAA): 108

## Campus Infrastructure (Honest Assessment)

- Permanent campus operational since ~2017; ongoing construction as of 2024
- Infrastructure is functional but still developing compared to older IITs
- Hostels exist on permanent campus; quality is reasonable, not comparable to IIT Delhi/Bombay
- Labs are operational; research equipment available for core research groups
- Internet connectivity: generally adequate for academic work; occasional disruptions
- Weather: harsh winters (Nov–Feb), heavy monsoon (Jul–Sep); power outages possible
- Road connectivity: NH-3 (Chandigarh–Manali); susceptible to landslides in monsoon
- No major city within 2-hour radius — this is a significant lifestyle constraint

- School of Mechanical & Materials Engineering (SMME)

- School of Basic Sciences (Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry)
- School of Humanities & Social Sciences

## Institute Rankings (Honest)

- NIRF Ranking 2023: ~50–60 range (Engineering category)
- NIRF varies year to year; not top-20 in engineering
- QS/THE rankings: Not individually ranked due to size/age
- Honest comparison: Stronger than most NITs in research output per faculty; weaker than IIT Delhi/Bombay/Madras/Kharagpur/Roorkee in placements and alumni network

## Key Differentiators vs Other Gen-2 IITs

| Factor | IIT Mandi | IIT Ropar | IIT Patna | IIT Jodhpur |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Location | Remote Himalayan | Punjab, better connectivity | Bihar, urban | Rajasthan, semi-urban |
| Research culture | Moderate-strong | Moderate | Moderate | Moderate |
| Placement ecosystem | Developing | Similar | Similar | Similar |
| Campus completion | ~80% | ~85% | ~75% | ~80% |
| Weather suitability | Extreme | Moderate | Extreme heat | Extreme heat/cold |

## Faculty Strength

- Total faculty: ~120–140 (approximate, 2023–24)
- Many faculty with PhDs from IITs/IISc/foreign universities
- Research publications growing year on year
- Some faculty with international collaboration (IIT Mandi iHub, FIST grants)
- Honest note: Faculty-to-student ratio is better than many NITs; PhD supervision quality varies by department

## iHub and Special Initiatives

- IIT Mandi iHub and HCI Foundation: Set up under NM-ICPS (National Mission on Interdisciplinary Cyber-Physical Systems)
- Focus: Human-Computer Interaction, assistive technology, mountainous terrain challenges
- Provides some funded internship opportunities for UG students
- Note: iHub is early-stage; not comparable to IIT Bombay's industry partnerships in scale

## Honest Positioning for Counseling

- IIT Mandi is a legitimate IIT with real research activity and decent placements for its size
- It is NOT equivalent to IIT Delhi, Bombay, Madras, Kharagpur, Roorkee in brand, alumni network, or placement volume
- It IS better than most NITs in research culture and IIT brand value for higher studies
- Location is a genuine constraint — students must be mentally prepared for isolation
- Best suited for students who: want IIT brand + are okay with smaller campus life + have specific research interests or are targeting higher studies
- Not ideal for students who: need strong city connectivity, large alumni networks for placements, or are purely placement-driven with top-tier targets
