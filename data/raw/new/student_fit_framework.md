# Student Fit Framework

Purpose: capture brief heuristics about which students succeed in which branches and who should avoid them.

Who should take `EE`
- Enjoys circuits, signals, and hardware labs.
- Comfortable with math (linear algebra, calculus) and circuit analysis.
- Interested in embedded/telecom/power/control applications or PSU paths.

Who should avoid `EE`
- Dislikes labs, abstract circuit math, or long-form theory without applied payoff.

Who should take `ME`
- Enjoys hands-on design, mechanics, fabrication, and system-level thinking.
- Comfortable with spatial reasoning and lab work.

Who should avoid `ME`
- Prefers abstract math and coding over physical systems, or dislikes workshop environments.

Who should take `CSE`
- Enjoys programming, problem-solving, and building software products.

Who should avoid `CSE`
- Disinterested in sustained coding practice or algorithmic thinking; wants purely hardware roles.

Who should take `DSE`
- Enjoys statistics, modeling, and applied machine learning; comfortable with math and experimentation.

Who should avoid `DSE`
- Prefers systems-level engineering or pure software engineering without data emphasis.

Usage notes
- These are heuristics — always cross-check against the student's stated goals (placements/PSU/grad school/startup) and appetite for practice.

End.
