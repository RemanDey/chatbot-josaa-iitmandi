# Academics and Policies at IIT Mandi — Fact Sheet

Verified academic policies, grading, and curriculum structure for undergraduate students.

## Academic System
- **Credit System:** IIT Mandi operates on a credit-token system. Students must complete a set minimum credit requirement by the end of their fourth year to graduate.
- **Grading:** The institute primarily follows a relative grading system, where a student's grade in a course is determined by their performance relative to the class average.
- **Attendance:** IIT Mandi officially mandates a minimum of 80% attendance in all courses.

## First-Year Curriculum
- The first-year B.Tech curriculum is designed to be largely common across all branches to provide a strong interdisciplinary foundation. 
- It includes core courses in Python programming, basic computing, mathematics, and physical sciences.

## Minors and Double Majors
- **Minor Degrees:** Students can pursue minor degrees in fields like Computer Science, Intelligent Systems, and more by completing a designated number of elective credits. There is typically no strict CGPA bar to opt for these minors, making them highly accessible to non-circuital branch students.
- **Double Major:** To pursue a Double Major, students generally need to maintain a CGPA of 8.0 or above and secure approvals from the respective departments.

## Branch Change Policy
- **Discontinued:** The branch change option at the end of the first year has been discontinued at IIT Mandi for recent batches. Students must pursue and graduate in the branch they are allocated during the JoSAA/CSAB counselling process.
