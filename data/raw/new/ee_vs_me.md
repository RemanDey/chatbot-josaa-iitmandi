# EE vs ME — Decision Notes

Purpose: concise comparison for counseling: Electrical & Electronics (EE) vs Mechanical (ME).

Core differences
- EE: focuses on circuits, signals, electronics, control, and power systems.
- ME: focuses on mechanics, thermodynamics, manufacturing, and system-level mechanical design.

Career & placement
- EE: more natural fit for electronics, power, controls, telecom, and embedded software roles.
- ME: better fit for core manufacturing, automotive, robotics, and product design roles; also increasingly present in automation/robotics software roles.

When recommend EE
- Student enjoys circuits, electronics, control theory, or wants PSU roles in power/utility sectors.

When recommend ME
- Student prefers tangible mechanical systems, design, manufacturing, or wants roles in robotics/automotive/manufacturing.

Cross-over notes
- Robotics and controls often accept both, but EE students may need stronger mechanical intuition for mechanical design tasks and ME students need control/signal familiarity for embedded systems.

Agent guidance
- Ask about lab comfort, hands-on vs theoretical preference, PSU interest, and desired industry sectors before choosing.

End.
# EE vs ME at IIT Mandi

**EE (Electrical Engineering):**
- More math, circuits, and electronics
- Best for PSU/core/government jobs
- Easier software transition than ME
- Higher placement % and packages than ME

**ME (Mechanical Engineering):**
- Focus on mechanics, design, manufacturing
- Best for GATE/PSU and core engineering
- Software transition is harder
- Lower placement % and packages than EE

**Summary:**
- EE is more versatile and has better placement/PSU options. ME is best for those passionate about core mechanical/design.
