# IIT Mandi — Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)

## 1. Branch Overview

- Full name: B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering
- Duration: 4 years (8 semesters)
- Department: School of Computing & Electrical Engineering (SCEE)
- Intake: ~40–50 students per batch (verify with official JoSAA seat matrix each year)
- This is the highest-demand branch at IIT Mandi; lowest closing rank in general category

## 2. Academic Difficulty

- **High difficulty** by IIT standards
- Theory-heavy in 1st/2nd year: Discrete Mathematics, Theory of Computation, Algorithms, Data Structures
- 3rd/4th year: elective-heavy; students can specialize
- Math requirement is substantial but less than EE or EP
- Programming workload: significant; assignments, lab work, projects
- GPA distribution: Relative grading means even capable students may see moderate GPA
- Difficulty is manageable for JEE-prepared students but requires consistent effort

## 3. Curriculum Reality

**Year 1 (Common + CS foundation):**
- Mathematics I & II (Calculus, Linear Algebra)
- Introduction to Programming (Python/C)
- Physics, Chemistry
- Engineering Drawing

**Year 2 (Core CS):**
- Data Structures & Algorithms
- Discrete Mathematics
- Computer Organization & Architecture
- Digital Logic Design
- Probability & Statistics
- Operating Systems (intro)

**Year 3 (Core + Electives):**
- Theory of Computation
- Compiler Design
- Database Management Systems
- Computer Networks
- Algorithm Design & Analysis
- Electives begin (AI, ML, Computer Vision, etc.)

**Year 4 (Electives + Project):**
- Advanced electives (NLP, Distributed Systems, Cryptography, etc.)
- B.Tech Project (2 semesters, mandatory)
- Minor thesis if minor program pursued

*Note: Exact course titles may vary; refer to IIT Mandi SCEE official curriculum page for current version.*

## 4. Math Intensity

- **Moderate-High**
- Linear Algebra: used in ML, Computer Vision, Graphics
- Probability & Statistics: used in ML, Systems
- Discrete Mathematics: used in Algorithms, Theory of Computation
- Calculus: used in optimization (ML focus)
- Not as math-heavy as EE or EP; heavier than pure software schools
- Students comfortable with JEE-level math will manage

## 5. Coding Adjacency

- **Very High** — this is the most coding-adjacent branch
- All core courses involve programming assignments
- Projects are code-heavy
- Students are expected to become proficient in C, C++, Python at minimum
- Java, web frameworks, databases: covered in coursework or self-study
- CP culture on campus: present but student-driven (see coding_culture.md)

## 6. Core Engineering Depth

- Low-to-moderate core engineering (in the hardware/circuit sense)
- Computer Architecture is core
- No circuits/power/mechanical content
- Software engineering, systems, algorithms are the "core" of this branch
- Students wanting hardware should look at EE

## 7. Software Transition Difficulty

- **Essentially no transition needed** — CSE IS software
- Students who keep up with DSA + system design can directly target SWE roles
- Competitive programming supplementation helps for top product companies
- ML/AI roles: require additional ML math and frameworks (TensorFlow, PyTorch)
- Backend engineering, frontend: coursework + self-learning path is clear

## 8. Placement Reality

- **Best placement outcomes among all IIT Mandi branches**
- Placement % for CSE: ~85–95% of registered students typically placed (uncertainty: varies by year)
- Package range: ₹12–40+ LPA; median approximately ₹18–25 LPA (estimate; official data not always published granularly)
- Some students receive international offers (40–80 LPA range) — small numbers
- Some students opt out for higher studies, startups, UPSC
- **Uncertainty disclaimer:** IIT Mandi does not publish granular branch-wise placement reports publicly in a standardized way; figures above are estimated from available information and peer accounts. Treat as approximate.

## 9. Recruiter Categories

**Tier 1 (Product/High-paying):**
- Microsoft, Amazon, Adobe, Google (rare), Walmart Global Tech, Samsung R&D, Qualcomm (occasionally)

**Tier 2 (Mid-tier product/service):**
- Infosys, Wipro, TCS (occasionally), Mphasis, Persistent Systems, Nagarro

**Tier 3 (Startups, domain-specific):**
- Various funded startups; depends heavily on year

**PSU/Government:**
- Limited direct CS-specific PSU roles; ISRO, DRDO projects possible via research path

*Note: Recruiter list changes every year. Do not treat this as a fixed list.*

## 10. Internship Opportunities

- 3rd year summer internship is standard
- Platforms used: LinkedIn, company portals, institute placement cell
- Industry internships at mid-tier companies are achievable for most CSE students
- Top-tier internships (Google, Microsoft, Goldman Sachs) are very competitive; small numbers
- Research internships (MHRD VAANI, DAAD, MITACS): accessible with good GPA + research output
- Summer research at IITs/IISc: possible with faculty recommendation
- PPOs from internships: happen; numbers small but meaningful

## 11. Research Areas

- Machine Learning, Deep Learning
- Computer Vision
- Natural Language Processing
- Distributed Computing
- Human-Computer Interaction (iHub)
- Algorithms and Theory (limited faculty strength here)
- IoT and Embedded Intelligence

## 12. Higher Studies Outlook

- **Good** — IIT brand helps significantly
- MS in USA/Canada/Europe: students do pursue; small but consistent numbers
- M.Tech at IIT Delhi, IIT Bombay, IIT Madras: competitive but achievable with 7.5+ CGPA
- PhD from IIT Mandi itself: possible; stipend + degree pathway
- GATE for CS: CSE students have clear alignment; preparation is feasible alongside coursework

## 13. PSU/GATE Optionality

- GATE CS: Directly applicable; CSE curriculum covers most GATE CS syllabus
- PSU through GATE: Oil India, GAIL, BHEL do NOT hire CS through GATE generally; ISRO is the main exception
- GATE for M.Tech: Primary PSU-adjacent route
- Government jobs via UPSC: Some students take this route (requires separate prep)

## 14. Startup/Tech Flexibility

- **Highest** among all branches at IIT Mandi
- Startup founding: CSE students have the most relevant skills
- Freelancing, consulting: possible with portfolio
- IIT Mandi Catalyst incubator: some student startup support
- Full-stack, ML, mobile development skills are startup-ready

## 15. Common Misconceptions

- "CSE at any IIT = automatic ₹30+ LPA package": False; median is lower, top packages are outliers
- "IIT Mandi CSE = IIT Delhi CSE outcomes": False; brand gap is real and affects placements
- "CGPA doesn't matter in CS": False; research positions and some companies do GPA filter
- "Competitive programming is mandatory for placements": Partially true — DSA is mandatory, Olympic-level CP is not

## 16. Honest Downsides

- IIT Mandi brand is weaker than Gen-1 IITs; recruiters notice
- Remote location limits networking with industry
- Fewer top-tier company visits compared to IIT Delhi/Bombay
- Alumni network thin (institute is young); mentorship for placements is peer-driven mostly
- Competitive pressure to "make it" to software is high; core CS theory may get deprioritized

## 17. Best Fit Student Profile

- JEE Advanced rank in IIT Mandi CSE range who cannot access higher-ranked IIT branches
- Students genuinely interested in software/ML/systems
- Students planning for higher studies (MS/PhD abroad or top IITs)
- Students comfortable with smaller cohort and self-driven career building
- Students who want IIT brand + are okay with remote campus

## 18. Weak Fit Student Profile

- Students expecting IIT Delhi-level placement ecosystem
- Students strongly preferring urban campus (would do better at IIT Hyderabad CSE if rank allows)
- Students not interested in software at all (should consider EE or different institute)
- Students needing large peer group for motivation

## 19. Comparison Notes

- **CSE vs DSE at IIT Mandi:** CSE has slightly stronger placement and brand; DSE is newer with data-focused curriculum. Prefer CSE if rank allows.
- **IIT Mandi CSE vs IIT Ropar CSE:** Similar placement tier; IIT Ropar has better connectivity (Punjab location). Preference depends on personal factors.
- **IIT Mandi CSE vs NIT Trichy CSE:** Complex tradeoff; NIT Trichy has stronger alumni network and urban location; IIT Mandi has IIT brand for higher studies. See mandi_vs_nitt comparison.

## 20. Final Counseling Interpretation

IIT Mandi CSE is the strongest branch at this institute. For students whose JEE Advanced rank falls in this range and cannot access CSE/higher branches at older IITs or better-connected Gen-2 IITs (Hyderabad, Gandhinagar), IIT Mandi CSE is a solid choice with legitimate software career pathways. The key honest constraint is the brand gap vs older IITs and the remote location. Students who are self-driven, coding-oriented, and open to smaller campus life will do well. Students comparing with NIT Trichy/NIT Warangal CSE should weigh IIT brand + research opportunity vs alumni network + location — there is no universally correct answer.
