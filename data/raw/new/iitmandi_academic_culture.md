# IIT Mandi — Academic Culture

## Grading System

- Follows 10-point CGPA scale (standard IIT system)
- Relative grading in most courses: grades are curved relative to class performance
- Absolute grading exists in some courses at faculty discretion
- GPA required for branch change: typically 8.0+ CGPA after first year (competitive)
- For PhD/MS admissions elsewhere: 7.5+ CGPA is generally minimum; 8.5+ is competitive for top institutes

## Academic Difficulty — Honest Assessment

- First year is common for all branches: Mathematics (Calculus, Linear Algebra), Physics, Chemistry, Programming (Python/C)
- Difficulty is genuine IIT-level: not comparable to NIT or private college workload
- Students from JEE Advanced with strong math backgrounds adapt better
- Students purely focused on JEE coaching without conceptual depth may struggle initially
- Second year onward: branch-specific courses become dominant; difficulty varies significantly by branch

## Semester Structure

- Two semesters per year (July–November, January–May)
- Credit-based system; typical course load: 20–25 credits/semester
- Continuous Assessment: Quizzes, Mid-sem, End-sem (typical split: 30-30-40 or 20-30-50)
- Lab courses: compulsory across all branches in relevant years
- Project courses: significant component in 3rd and 4th year

## Study Culture Reality

- Study culture is moderate-to-intense; not as cutthroat as older IITs reportedly
- Collaborative learning is more common than at some older IITs
- Library and lab access generally available 24/7
- Group study patterns common especially around exams
- Self-study vs lecture balance: lectures are important (not like some places where skipping is norm)
- Attendance policy: formally required; enforcement varies by faculty

## Academic Flexibility

- Minor programs available (e.g., Minor in Data Science, Minor in Management)
- Students can pursue electives from other departments in later semesters
- Audit courses possible
- Independent study/research credits available under faculty guidance
- Credit transfer for exchange programs (limited exchange opportunities exist)

## Faculty Teaching Quality — Honest

- Varies significantly by faculty member and department
- Some faculty are research-focused with less teaching emphasis
- Student feedback: generally mixed; some excellent teachers, some gaps
- CSE/EE departments have relatively stronger teaching feedback
- New faculty joining regularly; stability improving

## Research Participation for UG Students

- Summer research internships on campus possible under faculty (SURP-type)
- Final year B.Tech thesis/project is mandatory (2-semester, 8 credits typical)
- Some students publish papers from B.Tech projects (small percentage)
- IUAC, CSIR, SERB-funded projects occasionally take UG interns
- Quality of B.Tech project heavily depends on faculty supervisor chosen

## Academic Calendar Constraints

- Long winter break (~2 months: Dec–Jan) due to harsh Himalayan winters
- This winter break is useful for internships, online courses, competitive prep
- Monsoon season affects commute/activities but not academic calendar materially

## Common Academic Pitfalls

- Underestimating first-year math intensity — Linear Algebra and Calculus are rigorous
- Choosing too many electives without strategic focus
- Not engaging with research early (wasted opportunity given proximity to faculty)
- CGPA decline in 3rd/4th year due to overcommitment to clubs/competitions
- Not using the relatively low competition (vs older IITs) to stand out in research

## Peer Quality

- Peer quality determined by JEE Advanced rank: typically CRL 8,000–20,000+ depending on branch
- Students are genuinely capable but distribution is wider than older IITs
- Top students are comparable to mid-tier older IIT students
- The long tail extends further — diversity in preparation levels
- Peer learning benefit: moderate; self-driven learning matters more here than at IIT Bombay
