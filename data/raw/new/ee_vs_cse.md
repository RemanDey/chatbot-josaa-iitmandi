# EE vs CSE — Decision Notes

Purpose: compare Electrical & Electronics (EE) with Computer Science & Engineering (CSE) for counseling.

Core differences
- EE: hardware, circuits, signals, control systems.
- CSE: algorithms, software systems, databases, theory, software engineering practices.

Placement & career
- CSE: strongest direct path to software/product roles and large recruiter pools.
- EE: good for hardware-software roles, embedded, firmware, and domain-specific software; requires deliberate software upskilling for product roles.

When recommend CSE
- Student wants software as a primary career, enjoys programming and algorithms, and is willing to prepare for interviews.

When recommend EE
- Student prefers hardware, physical systems, or PSU/telecom/power sectors, or likes embedded systems that combine software+hardware.

Transition notes
- EE→software: feasible with 6–12 months of DSA + portfolio work; emphasize systems and networking courses.
- CSE→hardware roles: possible via electives, embedded projects, and internships but may require hardware lab experience.

Agent guidance
- Probe early for coding enjoyment and willingness to do structured DSA practice before suggesting CSE over EE.

End.
