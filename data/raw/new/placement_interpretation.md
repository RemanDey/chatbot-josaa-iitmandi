# Placement Interpretation

Purpose: help interpret placement statistics correctly to avoid misleading conclusions.

Median vs Average
- Median: middle value; robust to outliers — better for understanding a "typical" outcome in skewed distributions.
- Average (mean): sensitive to a few very high packages; can overstate central tendency when outliers exist.

Why placement % fluctuates
- Batch composition: ability and internship exposure vary year-to-year.
- Recruiter cycles: large recruitments (university drives, campus-specific partnerships) can spike placement rate one year.
- Economic conditions: macro hiring environment affects all branches.

Batch size effects
- Small cohorts can show high variance; single large hire or single large internship cohort can swing percentages considerably.

Why lower % ≠ dead branch
- Lower placement percentage often reflects cohort makeup, fewer students participating in placement, or strategic choices (many students pursuing grad school or startups). It does not by itself mean the branch is inferior.

Practical checklist for interpreting placement data
1. Check cohort size and participation rate.
2. Look at median and percentile spreads, not just mean and top offers.
3. Examine recruiter mix (product vs research vs domain companies).
4. Check internship conversion rates — strong predictor of placement outcomes.

Agent guidance
- When a user cites placement %, ask for cohort size, median/mean, and top-10% concentration before drawing strong conclusions.
